Multi-omics integration for sub-phenotyping of the spectrum diagnoses asthma and COPD

Sep 8, 2019

COPD and asthma are umbrella diagnoses, and current diagnosis based on clinical characteristics alone is not sufficient. Molecular descriptors of disease sub-phenotypes are desperately needed to stratify patient populations for optimal diagnosis and treatment. We aim to utilize our recently developed systems medicine framework to facilitate identification of molecularly distinct sub- phenotypes of them in two systematical multi-omics cohorts, to help stratify these heterogeneous populations into molecularly distinct entities.

Alterations in mRNA, microRNA, proteomes, metabolomes, and lipid mediators from multiple anatomical locations have been quantified in two clinical cohorts specifically designed for systems medicine investigations. Data will be integrated using newly developed computational systems biology approaches in multi-omics integration and disease module biomarker identification. We found that integration of 5-7 omics data-blocks enabled 100% correct classification of COPD diagnosis with groups as small as n=6 individuals, despite strong confounding effects of current smoking.

The stratification of the large spectrum of chronic obstructive lung diseases that today are housed under the umbrella diagnoses asthma and COPD represents a first, essential step to facilitate the development of new diagnostic tools and pharmaceutical targets for this debilitating spectrum of diseases, eventually reducing healthcare costs, improving quality of life, and saving lives.
